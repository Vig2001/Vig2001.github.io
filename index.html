I am a statistics PhD student at UCL, supervised by Dr. Brieuc Lehmaan and Prof. Karla Diaz Ordaz.

My research focuses on developing new methods and theory for benchmarking observational studies against randomised controlled trials (RCTs) and so is closely related to generalisability, data fusion (trial augmentation), and sensitivity analysis. I am passionate about exploring problems related to covariate shift. E.g. What happens when the target population is different from the source? Can we bound the bias arising from varying levels of covariate shift? Is it possible to create methods robust to this shift? ü§î

With the recent advancements and growing interest in AI, I am curious about how my research could inform robust and generalisable predictive models! ü§ñ

I had a very brief stint working as a junior actuary and then a slightly longer stint as a junior data scientist working on marketing mix modelling. Before that, I graduated from a MSci in Mathematics (with Statistics) from Imperial. In my free time I enjoy playing cricket, attempting to lift heavy weights, and failing to run quickly.
